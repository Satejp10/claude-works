Filling the reservoir, powering the state

Each year Koyna is drained to near-empty by pre-monsoon, then refilled to its 105 TMC brim in a matter of weeks. The same water then splits two ways — west through the turbines to the Arabian Sea, east to the fields and taps of the Krishna basin.

Two directions out of one lake

Koyna's quirk: turbine water leaves the basin permanently. So every unit generated is a unit the east side never sees — hence the fixed quota above.
West · Power

Water drops down the steep Western Ghats escarpment through the powerhouses. Monsoon = abundant water = peak generation season.

Method & caveats

Fill curve: gross-storage readings from daily 2026 monsoon reports (28 Jun low → 11 Jul); the dashed segment is an illustrative projection to full capacity, which Koyna typically reaches by 31 October.

Allocation: the ≈67.5 TMC westward power share reflects Koyna's fixed inter-state (Krishna) allocation; the east-side irrigation/drinking sub-split is approximate and directional — actual releases shift year to year, and in drought years drinking water is prioritised while power is cut first.

Grid mix: installed-capacity figures are firm — CEA/MNRE, February 2026 (large hydro 3,047 MW + small hydro 384 MW = 3,431 MW; Koyna 1,960 MW). Segments sum to ~60,574 MW; the ~330 MW "Other" reconciles to the CEA state total of 60,905 MW. The generation shares (○) are estimates — coal does the bulk of actual generation while solar/hydro run at low capacity factors (Koyna ≈21%), and hydro output swings with the monsoon, so its energy share moves year to year.
